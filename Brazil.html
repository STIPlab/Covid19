⇤ OECD Survey on the STI policy responses to Covid-19

 

Brazil


Updated on: 2020-03-31

Response
What arrangements, if any, do you have in place to ensure scientific advice informs national policy and decision making in relation to Covid-19? "Rede Vírus-MCTIC - Among the initiatives the Ministry of Science, Technology, Innovation and Communication has carried out to support the fight against Coronavirus (SARS-CoV-2; COVID-19), is the creation of an Experts Committee Rede Vírus-MCTIC (Virus-MCTIC Network) on Mar 11th 2020. The idea of creating this Network arose from a meeting held in early February 2020, with the aim of promoting a technical discussion of how science can help Brazil in tackling the problem of emerging viruses, especially the coronavirus. Scientists specialized in the area and representatives from the Ministry of Health participated in the debate. The Virus-MCTIC Network is already working to assist the Government in initial RD&I actions to face this pandemic, as well as in the definition of research priorities related to COVID-19.

The Network plans to use MCTIC Research Units as CERTIFIERS for local producers so that they can manufacture materials to combat COVID-19. These Research Units are spread across Brazil and can respect the distinct nature of Brazilian regions, enabling the use of local sanitizing products, such as alcohol, detergents, masks, moist towels, etc."
In what ways, if any, are you coordinating on Covid-19 STI responses at international level? Minister Marcos Pontes is representing Brazil in a working group established between Ministers, Chief Scientific Advisers or equivalent from the following countries: Australia, Brazil, Canada, Germany, India, Japan, New Zealand, South Korea, Singapore, United Kingdom, Spain, Portugal and USA. The objective of this working group is to share research results and information on how science can assist in the decisions and measures that we are taking to face the coronavirus that causes COVID-19.

Brazil has also joined UNESCO's Virtual Ministerial Dialogue on COVID-19 and Open Science.
Do you have dedicated arrangements in place for communicating science advice and for refuting misleading information to the public on Covid-19? "Fiocruz (Part of the Ministry of Health ) launched the ""COVID-19 | Information for Researchers"" page. In this space, scientists find links to the main sources of information about the new coronavirus, scientific and technical publications, situational maps and epidemiological reports. https://portal.fiocruz.br/coronavirus-20...

They also support multiple channels to provide information to the general population, including a new covid-19 specific Podcast - https://open.spotify.com/show/4JDOjmiNfS.... "

The Ministry of Health has a dedicated website on COVID-19 information (https://coronavirus.saude.gov.br/) as well as a mobile app (Coronavirus - SUS, available on iOS and Android) to provide information to the general public.
What new STI policy measures, if any, is your country taking to respond specifically to the Covid-19 crisis? On March 25th, an extraordinary credit of R$ 100 millon (~ USD 20 million) has been approved from the National Fund for Scientific and Technological Development (FNDCT - Fundo Nacional de Desenvolvimento Científico e Tecnológico). Funds will be used to promote research projects related to coping with the COVID-19 pandemic. This resource will be used for emergency support of projects (grants) within the scope of the priorities defined by the Virus-MCTIC Network, including:
* Development of diagnostic kits;
* Development of vaccine candidates;
* Use of advanced technologies for rapid selection of possible drugs;
* Large-scale sequencing and monitoring of mutations in SARS-Cov 2 (COVID-19) samples;
* Structuring a virus sample bank for scientific studies to understand the disease.

In addition, this resource will be used to support an Open Call (grant) in partnership with the Ministry of Health, to be launched via CNPq (National Reseach Council). The purpose of the call is to support STI in the areas of prevention, control and combating COVID-19 and other respiratory syndromes.

Overall priority is given to the development of a) medications and respective pre-clinical and clinical tests; b) vaccines and respective pre-clinical and clinical tests; c) innovation for the large scale production of ventilators and personal protective equipment (PPE) and their related systems; Research, development and innovation in diagnostic tests are also a priority; monitoring and sequencing the virus genome circulating in the country; and telemedicine tools.

To this end, the MCTIC will carry out: (i) direct contracting of projects, studies, technologies and innovations; (ii) public call(s) for contracting projects, studies, technologies and innovations; and (iii) coordination between organizations in the science, technology, innovation and communications system.

The National Postal Service Company (Correios) is acting as the logistics Partner for the Virus-MCTIC Network, being responsible for collecting viral material (new coronavirus and influenza) from selected laboratories and delivering them to other labs (in-kind contribution). These are being used in studies on contagion, proliferation of viruses and vaccines for diseases. Scientists working on the matter now have priority in receiving and sending research material, since time is crucial for the effectiveness of research with COVID-19.

The Ministry of Health Authorized the use of telemedicine to care for patients with coronavirus (March 23rd, 2020).

The Brazilian Company of Research and Industrial Innovation (EMBRAPII) has increased its share in non-refundable finance (previously up to 1/3 of the total funding) to innovation projects related to COVID-19. It is also providing additional funding of up to R$ 6 million (~US$ 1.2 million) in partnership with the Brazilian Micro and Small Business Support Service (SEBRAE) to fund innovation projects related to the diagnostics and treatment of COVID-19 to startups and SMEs. (https://embrapii.org.br/embrapii-e-sebra...)

The Sao Paulo Research Foundation (FAPESP) is providing additional funding of up to R$ 10 million (~US$ 2 million) to the redirection of research projects already underway to focus on COVID-19 (http://www.fapesp.br/en/14122).

FINEP, in partnership with FAPESP, has launched a joint call of R$ 20 million (~US$ 4 million) to fund innovation projects related to COVID-19 developed by SMEs in the state of Sao Paulo.
At national level, what mechanisms are you developing or relying upon to bring together different STI actors (researchers, industry, government, health sector, foundations, etc.) to effectively collaborate on responses to Covid-19? The main mechanism adopted so far is the RedeVirus MCTIC Experts Committee, described in Q1.A.
At international level, what mechanisms are you developing or relying upon to bring together different STI actors (researchers, industry, government, health sector, foundations, etc.) to effectively collaborate on responses to Covid-19? International initiatives are described in Q1.B.
What novel approaches, if any, is your country using to address the coronavirus crisis (e.g. use of machine learning, open science initiatives boosting access and sharing of data and research results, development and use of prediction models, etc.)? The call for proposals being launched with the extrordinary credit includes support for projects for large scale sequencing of viruses, use of advanced technologies (e.g. AI) for medicine selection and support of clinical trials, support for advanced vaccine development platforms (e.g. VLP) & Data Science projects for modeling and planning.
What impact on the STI system do you anticipate in the short-, medium- and long-term, and what measures are you implementing to address those? Short term:
- Loss of important human capital - Median age for researchers is typically higher than the general workforce - depending on the severity of the crisis;
- Disruption of ongoing research projects which require physical access to labs, other research facilities or medical facilities;
- Postponement of conferences.

Medium term:
- Higher public understanding on the importance of R&D;
- Higher reliance and confidence on remote work and collaboration arrangements, both for Research and work in general;
- Higher perception on the importance of access to scientific information and resources (articles, data, etc)
- Better availability of FAIR research data.

Long Term:
- Higher focus on health related research.
Is support of the STI system part of planned stimulus packages aimed at supporting the economy? The same bill that provided extraordinary credit of R$ 100 million (~ USD 20 million) for Covid-19 related R&D had R$ 62 millions (~USD 12 million) for Foreign Affairs, R$ 220 million (~USD 43 million) for Defense and R$ 3 billion (~USD 600 million) for direct transfers to citizens.

Other emergency economic measures are being adopted to support most affected sector and social groups. Measures are constantly updated at https://www.gov.br/planalto/pt-br.

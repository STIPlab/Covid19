OECD Survey on the STI policy responses to Covid-19

 

Denmark


Updated on: 2020-03-27

Response
What arrangements, if any, do you have in place to ensure scientific advice informs national policy and decision making in relation to Covid-19? Under the Danish Ministry of Health are several agencies, which employs health professionals and scientific personal (e.g. medicines agency and SSI). These agencies informs the minister of health, just like they are available for other ministries and agencies. As an example; the SSI participate at every press briefing held by the Danish authorities concerning convid-19.
In what ways, if any, are you coordinating on Covid-19 STI responses at international level? Denmark are member of several international forums, where covid-19 is coordinated.
Do you have dedicated arrangements in place for communicating science advice and for refuting misleading information to the public on Covid-19? The site Videnskab.dk, which is publicly funded, communicates on science in general, including convid-19. Denmark has also launched the site coronasmitte.dk, where all information from the Danish public authorities can be found.
What new STI policy measures, if any, is your country taking to respond specifically to the Covid-19 crisis? The Danish government has funded research projects, which can help to create new knowledge and new solutions in the fight against COVID-19.
In total DKK 50 million has been granted in a fast track model, where the deans of the four faculties of health sciences, the pro-rector of DTU and the academic director of the Statens Serum Institute (SSI) have been asked to submit proposals for covid-19 related research projects from the researchers at their own institutions.
The whole process took about one week, from call to relevant research projects were identified and granted money.
